Example 1:

https://junkcharts.typepad.com/junk_charts/2024/02/a-nice-plot-of-densities-but-whats-behind-the-colors.html

The Context

  1. The presentation compares air quality across 50 global capitals using PM2.5 data from IQAir's 2022 World Air Quality Report.
  1. Canberra, Australia, had the lowest PM2.5 level (2.8 μg/m³) in 2022.
  1. N’Djamena, Chad, recorded the highest PM2.5 level (nearly 90 μg/m³).
  1. Coastal capitals benefit from ocean breezes, improving air quality.
  1. Higher-income countries generally have better air quality.

The Pros

  1. Clear title: The chart has a clear and concise title, "COMPARING PM2.5 CONCENTRATIONS IN CAPITAL CITIES".
  1. Easy to understand symbols: The chart uses simple dots to represent the PM2.5 concentrations, which is easy to understand.
  1. Focus on capital cities: The chart focuses on PM2.5 concentrations in capital cities, which may interest policymakers and the public.

The Cons

  1. Unclear Ranking: Isn’t a guide to show the ranking of Pollutant within the city
  1. Colour Issue: The change in hues between particles is difficult to notice
  1. Double Referencing: The concentration is represented by the number of particles and the colour
  1. Overly Complex Charts: The representation is overly complex to represent the city's pollutant level

    A simple table or a Geographic heat map would have been sufficient

Possible Suggestions on design with examples

  1. Have an arrow or a single direction to guide the eyes. If it is ordered multi-directional, there are too many data points
  1. Represent the cities within a geographic instead of just numerical. This will provide spatial information too
  1. Represent the pollutant level by distinct colours and size of a single circle instead of having more partials and darker hues to represent higher pollutants

Example 2:

https://junkcharts.typepad.com/junk_charts/2024/02/neither-the-forest-nor-the-trees.html

The Context

The plot is from an article describing the top stocks driving the S&P 500

  1. S&P 500 surge driven by "Magnificent Seven": Alphabet, Amazon, Apple, Meta, Microsoft, Nvidia, and Tesla. They have risen nearly 117% since October 2022, outperforming the rest of the S&P 500.
  1. Market cap-weighted S&P 500 means large companies' performance has outsized influence.
  1. Magnificent Seven's gains contributed over 60% to the S&P 500's return last year. Smaller companies like Royal Caribbean, and General Electric saw gains but held less index weight.
  1. Big Tech dominance reflects market sentiment, but masks underlying turbulence.
  1. Highlights need to look beyond headline indices to understand market dynamics.

The Pros

  1. Clear Focus: The plot focuses on a specific and relevant topic, the top stocks driving the S&P 500's market value by highlighting market leaders
  1. Simple Design: The plot uses a straightforward treemaps design, making it easy for a broad audience to understand.
  1. Labelled Data: The plot includes labels for each company and its market value, allowing for easy comparison.

The Cons

  1. Lacks Context due to Limited Timeframe:  The plot doesn't provide context for the market value, such as how it compares to historical values or how these stocks performed relative to other sectors. This makes it difficult to understand trends or fluctuations.
  1. Limited Scope: The plot only focuses on the top 7 companies, excluding potentially relevant information about other players in the technology sector.
  1. Limited Additional Information: Besides market value, no other financial data points are included, like P/E ratio or dividend yield, which could provide a more nuanced picture.
  1. Not Interactive: The plot doesn't seem interactive, making it difficult to explore details or additional information about the data. Such as sorting by other financial data points or potentially looking into the top 10 or 15 companies instead of the top 7

Possible Suggestions on design with examples

  1. To add context, instead of a single treemap, we can have a side-by-side comparison or changes between years. A good example of this is Hans Rosling's Plotting life expectancy against income for every country since 1810

    https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jbkSRLYSojo&ab_channel=BBC

  1. Since we are looking into the top companies instead the all 500 companies. It is more interesting to colour-code the various companies by industry and highlight or have an outline for the top companies. This will show possible clustering or domination of one industry.

    https://finviz.com/map.ashx

    https://bulllabs.com/Home/SP500Map

    The above examples closely represent the suggestions. However, instead of changes in the weight of companies within the index, we can have the percentage indicated to show the industry or company contribution to the index fund.
