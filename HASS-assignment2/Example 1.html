Example 1:

  • The Context
    1. The presentation compares air quality across 50 global capitals using PM2.5 data from IQAir's 2022 World Air Quality Report.
    1. Canberra, Australia, had the lowest PM2.5 level (2.8 μg/m³) in 2022.
    1. N’Djamena, Chad, recorded the highest PM2.5 level (nearly 90 μg/m³).
    1. Coastal capitals benefit from ocean breezes, improving air quality.
    1. Higher-income countries generally have better air quality.

  • The Pros
    1. Clear title: The chart has a clear and concise title, "COMPARING PM2.5 CONCENTRATIONS IN CAPITAL CITIES".
    1. Easy to understand symbols: The chart uses simple dots to represent the PM2.5 concentrations, which is easy to understand.
    1. Focus on capital cities: The chart focuses on PM2.5 concentrations in capital cities, which may interest policymakers and the public.

  • The Cons
    1. Unclear Ranking: Isn’t a guide to show the ranking of Pollutant within the city
    1. Colour Issue: The change in hues between particles is difficult to notice
    1. Wasted Double Referencing: The concentration is represented by the number of particles and the colour, but this does now work hand in hand to further emphasize the poulltant level
    1. Overly Complex Charts: The representation is overly complex to represent the city's pollutant level. A simple table would been sufficient
  • Possible Suggestions on design with examples
    1. Have arrows to guide the eyes. If it is ordered multi-directional, there are too many data points
    1. Represent the cities within a geographic instead of just numerical. This will provide spatial information too
    1. Represent the pollutant level by distinct colours and size of a single circle instead of having more partials and darker hues to represent higher pollutants
